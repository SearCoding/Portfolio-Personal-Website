About Me

My name is Nasir Deshields, and I am an undergraduate student at Coppin State University, majoring in Computer Science. I am passionate about technology, problem-solving, and innovation in the STEM fields. With a strong foundation in programming and analytical thinking, I strive to develop solutions that are efficient, impactful, and user-oriented. Throughout my academic journey, I have gained experience in multiple programming languages, including Java, C, C++, HTML, and JavaScript, and I've worked on projects involving web development, application design, and system analysis. I am particularly interested in backend development, data structures, and building scalable software systems. Currently, I serve as the President of the Computer Science and Mathematics Club, where I help connect students with internship opportunities, organize technical workshops, and foster a collaborative learning environment. I have also worked as a STEM tutor, assisting students in mastering core concepts in computer science and mathematics. In addition, I've participated in research and professional development programs such as the Brookhaven National Laboratory WDTS Mini-Semester, where I explored interdisciplinary science and deepened my understanding of research applications in computing. I am actively seeking opportunities that allow me to apply my technical knowledge, contribute to meaningful projects, and continue growing as a developer and leader in the field of technology.
